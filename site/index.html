Convolutional Neural Network

Image Processing & Computer Vision

CNNs are specialized deep learning models designed for processing grid-like data such as images. They use convolutional layers to automatically and adaptively learn spatial hierarchies of features from input data. CNNs are the backbone of modern computer vision systems, powering applications from facial recognition to autonomous vehicles.

Recurrent Neural Network

Sequential Data Processing

RNNs are designed to recognize patterns in sequences of data, such as text, genomes, handwriting, or spoken words. Unlike feedforward neural networks, RNNs can use their internal state (memory) to process sequences of inputs, making them ideal for time-series analysis, natural language processing, and speech recognition.

Long Short-Term Memory

Advanced Sequence Modeling

LSTMs are a special kind of RNN capable of learning long-term dependencies. They were designed to avoid the vanishing gradient problem and can remember information for long periods, making them ideal for sequence prediction problems with long time lags. LSTMs are widely used in speech recognition, machine translation, and time-series forecasting.

Transformer

Attention-Based Models

Transformers are deep learning models that utilize self-attention mechanisms, differentially weighting the significance of each part of the input data. They have revolutionized natural language processing and are the foundation for state-of-the-art models like BERT, GPT, and T5. Transformers excel at tasks requiring understanding of context and relationships in data.
